Frequently Asked Questions — UAE Custom Clearances

1. Does your Moringa Powder comply with Dubai Municipality food safety entry rules?

Yes. Our export batches undergo systematic laboratory tests that comply with Dubai Municipality requirements and the ZAD/FIRS portal registration guidelines. We provide independent third-party laboratory COAs showing microbiological profiles and heavy metal clearances required at UAE points of entry.

2. How do you assist UAE importers with MoIAT and custom clearances?

IndiRoot Exims supplies comprehensive shipping document sets structured for hassle-free clearance through UAE Customs at Jebel Ali or Khalifa Port. This includes formal Commercial Invoices, Packing Lists, Certificates of Origin (COO), Phytosanitary Certificates from Indian authorities, and full batch-specific Certificates of Analysis (COA).

3. Is your export-grade moringa powder certified Halal for the Middle East market?

Our bulk moringa powder is a single-ingredient, 100% plant-derived botanical product free from animal elements or synthetic processing aids. For companies requiring formal documentation for retail packaging within the GCC, Halal compliance certificates are accessible upon request.

4. What is the minimum container allocation or MOQ for shipping to Jebel Ali Port?

Our baseline Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ) is 500 kg. However, to maximize sea freight cost efficiencies for shipments heading into Jebel Ali (Dubai) or Khalifa Port (Abu Dhabi), we highly recommend consolidating orders into 20ft Full Container Loads (FCL).

5. Can you provide custom high-mesh millings for UAE cosmetic manufacturers?

Yes. We process ultra-fine millings up to 200-mesh specifically designed for high solubility and smooth textures required by luxury cosmetic labs and personal care manufacturers in Dubai and Abu Dhabi.
